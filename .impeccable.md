## Design Context

### Users
Indian tech professionals (25-45) who've witnessed layoffs in their industry. Two primary emotional states:
1. **Anxious but proactive** — seen colleagues laid off, want protection now
2. **Curious explorers** — stable job, browsing during daytime, need to be convinced this category is real and legitimate

Primary viewing: daytime on laptop at work. Secondary: evening on phone at home.

### Brand Personality
**Reassuring, straightforward, human.**

Like a wise friend who happens to understand insurance. Not corporate. Not tech-bro. Not traditional Indian insurance. Uses plain language. Makes complex things simple. Inspires trust through clarity, not authority.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — daytime laptop viewing, reassuring emotional tone
- **Tone**: Warm minimalism. Clean but not cold. Approachable but not childish.
- **References**: Lemonade (friendly insurance), Stripe (quality execution), Linear (clean layout)
- **Anti-references**: GoDigit/PolicyBazaar (cluttered Indian insurance), generic dark SaaS with neon accents, any AI-generated look with glassmorphism/gradient text/glow effects
- **NOT**: Virat Kohli, yellow/orange, product grids, "AI-powered" buzzwords, startup templates

### Design Principles
1. **Simple, clean, premium** — feels expensive through restraint, not decoration. Every element earns its place.
2. **Warmth through whitespace** — generous breathing room and soft edges. No clutter, no noise.
3. **One strong accent** — single brand color used sparingly. Maximum impact through minimum use.
4. **Varied rhythm** — each section has its own spatial composition. No repeating patterns. Page feels curated, not templated.
5. **Trust through simplicity** — insurance is scary. Simple design = simple product = trustworthy.
6. **Creative yet restrained** — premium creative choices in layout, typography, and composition — NOT in effects, gradients, or decorations.
